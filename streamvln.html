StreamVLN

Qwen2-7B · SigLIP · 2025 · arXiv 2507.05240

A streaming navigation model built on the LLaVA-Video 7B model, designed to generate actions from continuous video at low latency.

Mechanism

StreamVLN keeps two context tracks, which the paper calls slow-fast. A fast-streaming track holds a sliding window of recent dialogue turns for responsive action generation; a slow-updating track compresses older visual states with a 3D-aware token-pruning strategy. The design targets stable low latency in real-world deployment.

Action output

The model emits the rare symbols ↑ ← → STOP; the harness parses them to action IDs and fixed magnitudes — forward 0.25 m, turn 15°.

Evaluation

On VLN-CE R2R Val-Unseen, StreamVLN reports a success rate of 56.9.

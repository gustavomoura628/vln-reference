OmniVLA

Llama-2-7B · OpenVLA-OFT · ICRA 2026 · arXiv 2509.19480

A goal-conditioned navigation model. It takes a goal as a 2D pose, a goal image, a language prompt, or a combination, and outputs a continuous trajectory.

Base modelOpenVLA (7B); action-head recipe from OpenVLA-OFT
Language modelLlama-2-7B
Vision encoderSigLIP + DINOv2
Goal modalities2D pose · goal image · language
Action output8-waypoint trajectory, each (dx, dy, hx, hy)
Training data> 9,500 h across 10 robot platforms
EvaluationReal-world (not VLN-CE); lang-goal SR 0.73

Mechanism

OmniVLA is goal-conditioned rather than instruction-following. It takes a goal in one of several modalities — a 2D pose, a goal image, or a language prompt — together with a current camera frame, and predicts a trajectory. It follows the OpenVLA-OFT recipe: a linear action head on the language-model output produces the trajectory chunk.

Action output

Each inference returns an 8-waypoint trajectory; every waypoint is (dx, dy, hx, hy) in the robot frame.

Evaluation

OmniVLA is evaluated on real-world navigation rather than the VLN-CE simulator, so it has no R2R/RxR success rate. With a language goal it reports a success rate of 0.73. It is trained on over 9,500 hours of data from 10 robot platforms.

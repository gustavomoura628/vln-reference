NaVILA

Llama-3-8B · SigLIP · RSS 2025 · arXiv 2412.04453

A two-level framework for legged-robot navigation: a vision-language-action model that emits actions in natural language, paired with a locomotion policy that executes them on the robot.

Language modelLlama-3-8B
Vision encoderSigLIP
FrameworkVILA-based
Input8-frame video clip: 1 current + 7 sampled history
Action outputNatural language, variable magnitude
R2R-CE SR (Val-Unseen)54.0

Mechanism

NaVILA separates navigation into two levels. A vision-language model reads the instruction and an 8-frame video, then outputs a mid-level action in natural language, for example “turn right 30 degrees”. A separate vision-based locomotion policy executes that action on the robot.

Action output

Each action is written as a short natural-language phrase with an explicit magnitude, such as a turn of a given number of degrees.

Evaluation

On VLN-CE R2R-CE Val-Unseen, NaVILA reports a success rate of 54.0. The paper states it outperforms prior methods on classic VLN benchmarks by over 17% in success rate.
